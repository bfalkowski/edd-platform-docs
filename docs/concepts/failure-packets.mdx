title: "Failure Packets"
description: "Evidence-backed units of diagnosis."
---

A `FailurePacket` is an evidence-backed unit of diagnosis.

It turns raw eval output into an actionable record: what failed, why it appears to have failed, what evidence supports that diagnosis, and what bounded fix should be considered.

## Two paths to a failure packet

**From the Proof Loop wizard** — when a run fails evaluation, the wizard guides the reviewer through naming the failure and creating a `FailurePacket` inline. This path is test-driven: a specific scenario and eval contract surfaced the issue.

**From Error Analysis** — when reviewing live Langfuse traces, recurring patterns are promoted to named `FailureMode` objects. A `FailureMode` can then produce a `FailurePacket` that is grounded in production behavior rather than a synthetic test.

## Recommended shape

A failure packet should include:

- Scenario: the case that exposed the issue.
- Expected behavior: what the agent should have done.
- Observed behavior: what the agent actually did.
- Evidence: trace IDs, eval results, judge outputs, screenshots, logs, or human notes.
- Failure category: the kind of failure being diagnosed.
- Suspected cause: the likely reason for the failure.
- Bounded fix recommendation: a specific change to try without expanding scope unnecessarily.

## Why packets are useful

Raw traces and scores are often too noisy to drive a fix directly. A failure packet gives reviewers a compact object they can inspect, discuss, and approve before creating a candidate `AgentVersion`.

## Bounded fixes

A bounded fix should be small enough to evaluate. Examples include adjusting an instruction, adding a missing constraint, changing a tool-selection rule, or adding a scenario-specific guardrail.

The platform should avoid treating a failure packet as a license for broad rewrites. The point is to make the next candidate version testable.
